Who is Shakespeare

William Shakespeare (1564–1616) was an English playwright, poet, and actor, widely
regarded as the greatest writer in the English language and one of the most influential
figures in world literature. Born in Stratford-upon-Avon, he wrote 39 plays, 154 sonnets,
and several narrative poems, exploring themes like love, power, betrayal, and human nature.

Main Characters in Romeo & Juliet Tragedy

1. Romeo Montague

Romeo is a romantic yet impulsive young nobleman of the Montague family, defined by his intense
emotions and idealism. Initially lovelorn over Rosaline, he instantly forgets her upon meeting Juliet,
showcasing his fickle passion. His reckless decisions—secretly marrying Juliet, killing Tybalt in vengeance,
and rashly poisoning himself upon (mistakenly) believing Juliet dead—catalyze the tragedy. Though poetic and loyal,
his lack of foresight underscores the play’s theme of youthful passion versus destructive haste

2. Juliet Capulet

Juliet is a 13-year-old girl whose transformation from obedient daughter to defiant woman drives
the play. Intelligent and courageous, she questions family loyalty after falling for Romeo, delivering iconic
soliloquies (e.g., “What’s in a name?”). Her fake suicide to escape marrying Paris—and eventual real suicide
upon finding Romeo dead—highlights her desperation in a world that denies her agency. Juliet embodies the
clash between individual desire and oppressive societal norms.

3.Friar Laurence

A well-meaning but flawed Franciscan friar, he acts as Romeo’s mentor and secretly marries the lovers,
hoping to end the feud. His misguided plans (the sleeping potion for Juliet) stem from optimism but spiral into disaster.
His late arrival to Juliet’s tomb seals their fates, making him a symbol of good intentions undone by poor judgment.

4.Mercutio

Romeo’s charismatic, quick-witted best friend, Mercutio is a foil to Romeo’s romanticism.
His bawdy humor and “Queen Mab”
speech reveal a cynical view of love. When Tybalt kills him, Mercutio curses both houses
(“A plague o’ both your houses!”), turning < the play from comedy to tragedy. His death exposes the feud’s pointless brutality.

5.Tybalt

Juliet’s hot-headed cousin, nicknamed “Prince of Cats” for his dueling skill. His hatred for Montagues
fuels key conflicts: he provokes Romeo at the ball and kills Mercutio, triggering Romeo’s revenge.
Tybalt represents generational vendettas that doom the young.
